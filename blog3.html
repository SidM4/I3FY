I3FY - The Mid-Point Revisited

The pressure is hitting now.. Going into overdrive..It is currently the 22nd of August. 5 days of countdown...

105 Views

I feel like things are mostly ready but I have some time to revisit what I have done so far.


One of the most important parts of a web site development is an active feedback cycle. Like our assessments at school which checks our understanding of the topics and gives us opportunity to improve through the year, the feedback cycle is crucial to understand what the viewer feels of the website and gain the input to improve. To be able to get the right kind of feedback, it is also important to find the people who should give the feedback. Given I was developing the website as a newbie learner, it was truly clear to me that it will not be comparable to the professional training sites, but at the same time, I wanted to make it as good as I could. Hence, I decided I should also get feedback from both newbies like me as well as someone who understands the concepts very well. And on top of that I had anyways got my coach who would be the best of both.

In my first plan, I had not factored periodic feedback activities, but after the first round my proud display, I realized the more frequent feedback I get, the lesser chances of me tripping up towards the end. I added a twice a week feedback capture sessions with my friend (an avid learner like me), my coach (the expert) and my kid brother (the newbie). Each of these had a distinct perspective on the website ranging from look and feel to depth of content to the navigation flows.

It was clear that I could not address all the expectations within the project, and I had to get to a mid-way path where I had to skip some comments as those needed elaborate coding (on areas I had not learnt), technical dependencies (aspects needing hosting and involved $$$) or downright whacky (giving away freebies for subscribing!!)... This experience of deciding on feedback helped me learn “scope management”
